Our projects

Check out what we have been working on, if you would like any more information get in touch here.

Jayden Newman - Allay

×

Allay is a small hand gardening tool attachment that presents a new grip, providing the option to hold the tool in different positions, as well as absorbing any unexpected shock experienced from impact with harder debris. This new grip promotes healthy wrist positioning throughout different gardening tasks and allows the user to confidently adapt to different gardening environments.

Cassie Aspden - B. Ligh

×

The Naturalights Project focuses on the health and wellbeing implications of working in a windowless environment. This has resulted in the B.Light: A Biophilic Design for windowless home or office spaces. With the use of Arduino Technology, a dimming effect is set to the time of day to restore the sense of time passing.

Saxon Brewer-Marchant - Kemi

×

Kemi is a multi-generational, chameleon puzzle. It utilises high quality materials and a blend of traditional and modern manufacturing techniques. Kemi exercises cognitive and creative development, with the individual puzzle pieces snapping together to create a 3D chameleon skeleton.

Nathaniel Jones - Labyrinth

×

Labyrinth is a tilting marble run designed to educate children about materials and recycling through play. It challenges children to sort materials through the layered tracks in order to recycle the materials. It also promotes teamwork and the development of fine motor skills through the tilting motion needed to move the balls along the path.

Arthur Holt - Flex

×

Flex is a freestanding workspace unit designed for people who use a wheelchair. It can be made to a custom height and used as both a desk and a kitchen prep area. There is space for basic cooking equipment, modular cooking tools, groceries and desk storage.

Luke Samson Hill - Penarth Pack

×

The Penarth pack combines two styles to create a functional bag for both the commuter and the explorer. The stylish exterior blends with the city aesthetic while its expandability creates a practical backpack for weekends in the great outdoors.

Charles Clarke - Woodland Waste

×

The Woodland Waste is a collaboration between Charles Clarke, Tom Raffield, The Eden Project and Tate and Harmer Architects aiming to help educate and increase awareness about sustainability. A mosaic of unique wooden veneer tiles, from the waste stream of Tom Raffield’s lighting business, is illuminated by a warm glowing LED light, highlighting the natural colours and shapes within the wood.

Connie Rudd - bU

×

A mindfulness craft kit to help girls build up their self-esteem and empower them to do what they want. For every kit that is sold, a portion of the profits go towards the charity ‘Change’ who have a campaign in empowering women in disadvantaged countries.

Elliot Lunn - FORMiT

×

FORMiT is a project for designers, makers and educators. It is an open source recycling tool for creating 3D objects from recycled paper pulp. Paper is an easily recycled and a versatile material, the aim is that makers and designers adopt the processes from this project and start experimenting with this material themselves. find out more on the FORMiT website

Chlöe Davies - It Matters

×

It Matters is a period pain compression garment designed for women to make them feel supported, empowered and attractive at a time when they can feel vulnerable and in pain, ‘Around 1 in every 20 women have symptoms that are severe enough to stop them living their normal lives.’ (NHS England) It Matters is part of the campaign to promote period positivity.

Drew Spathaky - The Tribute Tray

×

The Tribute Tray is a simple solution to the decline in social values within bars and pubs throughout England. It aids the purchase of a round of drinks for up to 5 people by allowing one person to comfortably carry all the drinks and reduce the chance of spillage. Once at the table, the tray can be used to hold phones, and keep them from distracting the group. The other part of the tray then becomes 2 different games for the group to play.

Wiktoria Boniecka - Modulaa

×

Modulaa is a motorbike textile gear that uses the latest technology in abrasion resistance and focuses on cutting the waste by making it modular, so that in the event of a crash, the damaged part of the jacket could be replaced.

Chloe Davies - Tiny Tor

×

Tiny Tor is a dual-purpose product that is developed to harness the health benefits of sheepskin, as well as championing the material qualities, traditional craft, upholstery techniques and applications of wool. Incorporating woven woollen yarn, natural felt, carded wool padding and the sheepskin itself.

Charles Clarke - Pendler

×

Pendler is a bicycle stand helping retailers make best use of their space by providing marketing opportunities and branding within a compact floor area. Pendler aims to help make cycling more appealing and accessible to a wider audience.

Jayden Newman - Helix

×

Conventional agriculture is becoming increasingly under pressure, and more and more of us are living together, particularly in urban environments. The Helix is a product in which families could interact, learn and produce their own fresh food inside their own homes. With great focus towards urban homes, the Helix home grower is designed for use inside existing kitchens.

Stefan Hewitt - Omne

×

Omne is a combination between a chopping board and kitchen knife that primarily utilises leverage to enhance an individual’s cutting power and ease. Designed to be inclusive of those with low dexterity, strength and control, its many features make it a useful kitchen addition for any generation of the family.

Skye Algera - Caring for Carers

×

Caring for Carers focuses on the wellbeing and mental health of the unpaid family caregiver taking care of a loved one with early or mid-stage dementia. The product makes use of user input to allow caregivers to track their mental and physical wellbeing, allowing them to monitor their own health alongside that of their loved one.

Drew Spathaky - Alex Light

×

The Alex light is an adaptable desk mood lamp which aims to reduce the prevalence of migraine attacks caused by physical triggers. The movement of the light acts as a timepiece which allows the user to plan their day on its surface to reduce stress, which is the main cause of migraines.

Cassie Aspden - The Tired Ones

×

The Tired Ones have been designed to tackle the problem of separation anxiety by incorporating the scent of a parent onto a child’s toy, as well as reinforcing a routine before bed, to enhance a better night’s sleep.

Connie Rudd - Hex

×

Hex is an interactive stand for outdoor spaces to encourage a connection between people and place. It will be implemented in spaces around the country, towns and parks. The hexagon pieces are taken out and used to explore the space.

Arthur Holt - life stories

×

Life Stories is about immersing people who have dementia and their families in memories. It provides people with a positive activity, rekindling relationships and helping individuals learn about a person before their dementia journey.

Nathaniel Jones - Kor

×

Kor is a kitchen appliance suite designed for rented and small space accommodation. This modular design combines an induction hob with a multi-speed motor to give the functionality of traditional kitchen appliances, but in a much smaller footprint.

Skye Algera - Stori

×

‘Stori’ focuses on empowering the Cornish community by supporting local business and celebrating Cornish art, culture, and heritage. The project consists of four automata scenes that describe and illustrate historical local business St Austell Brewery’s making their signature pale ale; Tribute.

John Ragadio - Trib Col

×

Trib Col is a three-piece furniture collection that aims to minimise the congestion in the bar environment. The seating products allow customers to move around the pub to socialise with different groups of people without the need for a conventional table.

Beni Leung - Lumba

×

Lumba is a bag design that considers ergonomics and aesthetics, transferring the weight from the bag to the lumbar area in order to reduce the pressure on the shoulders. The waistband component keeps the bag close to the user’s body for greater support.

Daniel Vorley - NAVA

×

With the emergence of automated technology in vehicles, one of society’s major hurdles is our trust that these systems will improve our driving experience in safety, comfort and sustainability. By tracking a set of internal stimuli, NAVA determines the fatigue or stress levels in the driver and automatically takes control when they leave a safe threshold.

Francesco Gentili - Aria

×

Aria is a meditation aid for beginners that are having difficulty learning meditation techniques, as well as intermediates that are trying to improve. Aria expands and contracts so that the user has an external stimulus to concentrate on and use as the meditation object.

Jeanet Wong - Ease

×

Ease is designed for autistic adolescents, autism is considered as a “lifelong developmental disability that affects how people perceive the world and interact with others” (NHS)”. This product allows those with anxiety to be calmed and focused in distracting environments.

Ollie Kidner - Reshoevenate

×

Reshoevenate is a closed-loop response to global footwear wastage problems. Utilising non virgin materials and intuitive user-centered design, a Reshoevenate shoe can be easily and quickly disassembled into mono material groups for recycling and remanufacture.

Stuart King - Peninsula

×

Peninsula aims to highlight a viable alternative to the current surfboard manufacturing methods that perform to the same standard or better than a PU or EPS constructed surfboard.

Ashu Sharma - Picture Puppets

×

There are currently 15,000 children with juvenile arthritis in the United Kingdom. Picture Puppets have been created to help these children reconnect with the joy of colouring in, with crayons. The finger puppet is very simple and easy to use and has a front finger support which helps individuals with arthritis.
